# NNN - Short title of the decision

- **Status:** Proposed
- **Date:** YYYY-MM-DD
- **Deciders:** names or roles

## Context

What problem are we solving? What forces are at play - technical constraints, business requirements, prior decisions, alternatives considered? Describe the situation as it stands before the decision is made. Be concrete; future readers will not remember the surrounding conversation.

## Decision

State the decision in one or two sentences, in the active voice. "We will use X for Y." Then expand with the specifics needed to act on it.

## Consequences

What becomes easier as a result? What becomes harder? What is now off-limits or requires a new ADR to revisit? List both the positive and the negative - an honest consequences section is what makes the ADR useful later.

## Alternatives considered

Briefly: what else was on the table, and why was it rejected? One short paragraph per alternative is enough. This is the section future readers will check when they're tempted to re-propose a rejected option.
